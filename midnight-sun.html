midnight sun solar car team

driver/center console displays

I am currently leading the user experience design for the dashboard of the solar car. I keep two goals in mind while designing. The overall goal for the solar race car is to be as efficient yet consumer-friendly as possible. The goal for the interior design is to support a driving experience that is safe, intuitive, and comfortable for drivers and passengers of the car. Because this project is in progress, this page is not yet complete and contains my work and thought processes so far!

THE PROCESS (SO FAR)

What’s involved?

Before designing anything, the first step was to know what was necessary for the interior following American Solar Challenge regulations. To make sure we included all the components, we asked about the experiences of drivers and passengers of our previous solar car, MSXII, and used sensory inputs/outputs as a guide to create a morphological chart. The chart, divided into subsystems and broken down into specific functions, was used to brainstorm all possible means of satisfying the functions. We narrowed the options to 1-2 for each function after analyzing different combinations and eliminating unviable choices.

After narrowing down some solutions, I grouped the components into sections to see how they would work together. Sketching and discussing some of these ideas made it clear that some choices were not feasible or intuitive, and were discarded early in the process.

I drew more detailed sketches of the dashboard to explore the options for different functions, allowing me to diverge from standard car designs and devise a solution that best fits the drivers’ needs.

On the digital side, the information on the dashboard screen contents was minimized to display the essential driver information. Although a design like in the screen below is geared towards a potential consumer, some information like the state of charge (SOC) may have to be revisualized for greater accuracy, as the electrical measurements may not translate fully into common visuals.

Challenges

Because the car is being built in tandem with the other electrical, mechanical, and firmware teams, the interior could be affected by certain changes made to the initial car plan. Constant team communication and prioritization is key to prevent any conflicting designs.

Next steps

There is still much work to do on the user experience side of the solar car-building process. Some current plans include:
- Creating physical prototypes to test usability of screens/font sizes
- Finalizing software and design with the electrical and firmware teams
- Designing the user interface for the dashboard and center console screens
